© 2026 Mayank Dotiyal · RIES, Hokkaido University
Engineering the atomic lattice of vanadium dioxide (VO₂) thin films — modulating chemical strain and dopant chemistry to tune the semiconductor–to–metal transition for energy-efficient smart windows.

Working within the laboratory of Prof. Hiromichi Ohta at RIES. My research bridges RF magnetron sputtering and wet-chemical synthesis with first-principles Density Functional Theory (DFT) utilizing VASP and Quantum ESPRESSO to correlate synthesis conditions with structure, defects, and functional properties in inorganic oxide systems.

Research Matrix

Doctoral Dissertation · IIT Gandhinagar

Chemical Strain-Induced Structural & MIT in VO₂ Thin Films

Substituted V4+ lattice sites with larger-radius, higher-valency cationic dopants to depress the transition temperature toward near-room-temperature levels. Synthesized via RF magnetron sputtering and elucidated through first-principles DFT across unstrained and strained M₁/R polymorphs.

Outcomes: 3 Peer-Reviewed Papers · 2 Indian Patents
Applied Technology

Thermochromic Smart Windows

Translating strain- and dopant-tuned VO₂ films into visibly transparent, high-stability heat-insulation coatings for energy-efficient architecture.

Grant: Tata Steel MaterialNEXT4.0
Ab Initio Theory

First-Principles Computation

Modeling thermodynamic stability, formation energies, and electronic density of states (VASP, Quantum ESPRESSO) to elucidate substitution-induced phase transitions.

Core Focus: Electronic Band Modulation
Master's Thesis · NIT Hamirpur

Surfactant-Controlled Defect & Strain Chemistry in Cr₂O₃ Nanoparticles

Modulated anionic (SDS) and cationic (CTAB) surfactant concentrations during wet-chemical co-precipitation to study lattice strain, optical defect states, and establish the inverse relationship between Urbach energy and optical bandgap.

Outcomes: 1 Book Chapter (Springer) · 1 Conference Proceeding
